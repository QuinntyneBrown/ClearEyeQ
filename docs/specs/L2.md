# ClearEyeQ — L2 Requirements (Design)

Breakpoints: **XS** (<375px), **SM** (375–639px), **MD** (640–1023px), **LG** (1024px+)

---

## L1-1: Onboarding & Authentication

### L2-1.1: Splash / Welcome Screen
Design a branded splash screen with ClearEyeQ logo, tagline ("Your eyes deserve superintelligence"), and a Get Started CTA button.
- **AC:** Logo centered, tagline visible, single CTA button. On XS/SM: full-bleed vertical layout. On MD: centered card. On LG: split layout with hero illustration left, content right.

### L2-1.2: Sign-Up Screen
Design sign-up with email, password, name fields, social auth buttons (Apple, Google), and terms checkbox.
- **AC:** Stacked form on XS/SM. Side-by-side fields on MD/LG. Social auth buttons are equal width. Error states shown inline beneath each field.

### L2-1.3: Login Screen
Design login with email/password fields, "Forgot password?" link, and login button.
- **AC:** Same responsive behavior as sign-up. Forgot-password link is right-aligned below password field.

### L2-1.4: Profile Setup / Permissions
Design a multi-step setup wizard: profile photo, health integrations (HealthKit, Google Fit, Oura), camera permission, notification permission.
- **AC:** Step indicator at top. One step per screen on XS/SM. Grouped steps on MD/LG. Each step has a primary action button and a skip option.

---

## L1-2: Dashboard & Home Screen

### L2-2.1: Eye Health Score Card
Design a prominent card showing current eye health score (0–100) with circular progress indicator, color-coded (green/yellow/red).
- **AC:** Score card is hero element at top. On XS: full-width card. On SM: full-width with padding. On MD: half-width left-aligned. On LG: one-third width in a grid.

### L2-2.2: Recent Scan Summary
Design a card showing last scan timestamp, thumbnail of scanned eye, redness level, and a "Scan Now" button.
- **AC:** Horizontal card with thumbnail left, text right on SM+. Stacked on XS. "Scan Now" button is prominent primary style.

### L2-2.3: Environmental Context Cards
Design cards for AQI, pollen count, humidity, UV index, and screen time — each with icon, value, and severity indicator.
- **AC:** Horizontal scroll row on XS/SM. 2-column grid on MD. 4-column grid on LG. Each card has icon, label, value, and color-coded badge.

### L2-2.4: Quick Action Bar
Design a quick-action area with buttons: Scan, Log Symptoms, View Trends, Get Forecast.
- **AC:** Fixed bottom bar on XS/SM with 4 icon+label buttons. Horizontal button row on MD. Integrated into sidebar on LG.

### L2-2.5: Daily Tip / Insight Banner
Design a dismissible banner showing a daily personalized tip or insight.
- **AC:** Full-width banner below score card. Includes icon, text, and dismiss (X) button. Collapses to single line on XS.

## L1-3: Smart Eye Scan Flow

### L2-3.1: Camera Viewfinder with AI Guide
Design the scan capture screen with camera preview, eye-alignment overlay (oval guide), positioning feedback text, and capture button.
- **AC:** Full-screen camera on XS/SM with overlay and bottom capture button. On MD/LG: camera in center panel with instructions sidebar. Oval guide has animated pulse when alignment is good.

### L2-3.2: Scan Progress & Processing
Design a processing screen shown after capture with progress indicator, "Analyzing your eye…" message, and animated graphic.
- **AC:** Centered content on all breakpoints. Circular progress bar with percentage. Subtle animated eye illustration.

### L2-3.3: Scan Results Summary
Design the results screen showing redness score, comparison to previous scan, condition flags, and action buttons (View Details, Rescan, Share with Doctor).
- **AC:** Score prominently displayed at top. Delta indicator (↑↓) vs last scan. Condition flags as chips/badges. Action buttons stacked on XS/SM, row on MD/LG.

---

## L1-4: Eye Health Timeline & Trends

### L2-4.1: Redness Score Timeline Chart
Design a line chart showing redness score over time (7d, 30d, 90d, 1y toggle).
- **AC:** Full-width chart on all breakpoints. Time-range toggle tabs above chart. Touch-friendly data points on XS/SM. Hover tooltips on MD/LG.

### L2-4.2: Environmental Correlation View
Design an overlay/multi-line chart correlating redness with environmental factors (AQI, pollen, sleep, screen time).
- **AC:** Toggle checkboxes for each factor. Legend visible. On XS/SM: legend below chart. On MD/LG: legend beside chart.

### L2-4.3: Pattern Insight Cards
Design insight cards like "Your redness spikes on days with high pollen + low sleep" with supporting mini-chart.
- **AC:** Card list below main chart. Each card has icon, insight text, confidence badge, and mini sparkline. 1 column on XS/SM, 2 on MD, 3 on LG.

---

## L1-5: Diagnosis & Root Cause Report

### L2-5.1: Differential Diagnosis List
Design a screen listing potential diagnoses with condition name, confidence percentage bar, and severity badge.
- **AC:** Sorted by confidence descending. Each row: condition name, percentage bar, severity chip (mild/moderate/severe). Tappable to expand. Full-width list on all breakpoints.

### L2-5.2: Causal Graph Visualization
Design an interactive causal graph showing how factors (sleep, AQI, medications, stress) connect to the diagnosed condition.
- **AC:** Node-and-edge graph on MD/LG. Simplified vertical flow diagram on XS/SM. Nodes are tappable to show detail popover.

### L2-5.3: Condition Detail Screen
Design a detail screen for a specific condition showing description, evidence from user's data, recommended actions, and medical references.
- **AC:** Scrollable single-column layout on XS/SM. Two-column layout on MD/LG (info left, evidence right). "Learn More" expandable sections.

---

## L1-6: Treatment & Action Plans

### L2-6.1: Active Treatment Plan Overview
Design a screen showing the current treatment protocol with phases, progress bar, and next-action card.
- **AC:** Phase timeline at top (horizontal stepper). Current phase highlighted. Next-action card prominent below. Full-width on XS/SM, centered card on MD/LG.

### L2-6.2: Intervention Cards (Medication, Behavioral, Environmental)
Design cards for each intervention type: medication (name, dosage, schedule), behavioral (screen breaks, hydration), environmental (humidifier, lighting).
- **AC:** Icon-coded by type (pill, activity, home). Stacked list on XS/SM. 2-column grid on MD. 3-column on LG. Each card has toggle for completion tracking.

### L2-6.3: Treatment Progress Tracker
Design a progress view showing treatment efficacy over time with before/after scan comparisons.
- **AC:** Side-by-side eye scan comparison. Redness score delta. Line chart of score improvement. On XS: stacked before/after. On SM+: side-by-side.

### L2-6.4: Specialist Referral Card
Design an escalation card shown when AI treatment hasn't met thresholds, with referral info and one-tap booking.
- **AC:** Alert-styled card with warning icon, explanation text, doctor info, and "Book Appointment" primary button. Prominent placement at top of treatment screen.

---

## L1-7: Predictive Alerts & Notifications

### L2-7.1: 72-Hour Forecast Banner
Design a forecast banner showing predicted redness levels for the next 3 days with contributing factors.
- **AC:** Horizontal 3-day forecast cards (like weather). Each day shows predicted score, primary factor icon, and risk level. Scrollable on XS, row on SM+.

### L2-7.2: Flare-Up Alert Dialog
Design a modal/dialog warning of imminent flare-up with cause, severity prediction, and preventive actions.
- **AC:** Centered modal with warning icon, title, body text, action list, and "Got it" / "Snooze" buttons. Full-screen sheet on XS, centered modal on SM+.

### L2-7.3: Treatment Reminder Notification
Design push notification and in-app reminder cards for medication, eye drops, screen breaks, and hydration.
- **AC:** In-app: toast-style at top with icon, text, action button. Swipe to dismiss on XS/SM. Auto-dismiss with manual close on MD/LG.

### L2-7.4: Specialist Referral Alert
Design an urgent alert for conditions flagged as potentially sight-threatening, requiring specialist review within 24 hours.
- **AC:** Red-accented alert card/dialog with urgency icon, condition name, recommendation text, and "Find Specialist" CTA. Cannot be dismissed without acknowledgment.

---

## L1-8: Settings, Profile & Data Management

### L2-8.1: Profile Screen
Design a profile screen with avatar, name, email, subscription tier badge, and edit button.
- **AC:** Avatar and name hero section at top. Info rows below. Edit button top-right. Consistent layout across all breakpoints.

### L2-8.2: Connected Apps & Devices
Design a list of health integrations (HealthKit, Google Fit, Oura, Whoop, CPAP) with connection status and toggle.
- **AC:** Each row: app icon, name, status badge (connected/disconnected), and toggle/connect button. Full-width list on all breakpoints.

### L2-8.3: Privacy & Data Controls
Design privacy settings with toggles for passive monitoring, anonymized data sharing, and buttons for data export and account deletion.
- **AC:** Grouped sections with section headers. Toggle rows. "Export My Data" and "Delete Account" are distinct destructive-styled buttons at bottom.

### L2-8.4: Subscription & Billing
Design a subscription management screen showing current plan, upgrade options (Free, Pro $9.99, Premium $29.99, Autonomous $99.99), and billing history.
- **AC:** Current plan highlighted card at top. Plan comparison cards below. On XS/SM: stacked cards. On MD: 2-column. On LG: 4-column row. Each card has tier name, price, feature bullets, CTA.

### L2-8.5: Accessibility Settings
Design accessibility options including text size slider, high contrast toggle, voice-guided scan toggle, and language selector.
- **AC:** Grouped setting rows. Text size has live preview. Language selector is dropdown/picker. All interactive elements meet WCAG 2.2 AA touch targets (44x44px min).

## L1-9: Shared UI Components & Design System

### L2-9.1: Button System
Design all button variants: primary, secondary, tertiary/ghost, destructive, disabled — in small, medium, large sizes with icon+label options.
- **AC:** All buttons shown in a component grid. Each variant has default, hover, pressed, and disabled states. Border radius, padding, and font sizes documented. Responsive sizing: small on XS, medium on SM/MD, large on LG.

### L2-9.2: Dialog / Modal System
Design modal variants: confirmation, destructive action, informational, and form input — with overlay backdrop.
- **AC:** Each dialog type shown. Has title, body, and 1–2 action buttons. Full-screen sheet on XS. Centered card modal on SM+. Close button (X) top-right.

### L2-9.3: Toast & Snackbar Notifications
Design toast/snackbar for success, error, warning, and info messages.
- **AC:** Appears at top on mobile (XS/SM), top-right on MD/LG. Color-coded left border. Icon, message text, optional action link, dismiss button. Auto-dismiss after 5s.

### L2-9.4: Navigation Components
Design bottom tab bar (XS/SM), side navigation rail (MD), and full sidebar navigation (LG) with icons and labels for: Home, Scan, Timeline, Treatment, Settings.
- **AC:** Bottom bar: 5 icons with labels, active state highlighted. Rail: icons only with tooltip on hover. Sidebar: icons + labels, collapsible. Active route visually distinct.

### L2-9.5: Form Inputs & Controls
Design text inputs, dropdowns, toggles, checkboxes, radio buttons, sliders, and date pickers in default, focus, error, and disabled states.
- **AC:** All input types shown in a component grid with all states. Labels above inputs. Error messages below in red. 44px minimum touch targets on mobile.

### L2-9.6: Card Components
Design card variants: stat card, action card, insight card, alert card — with consistent border radius, shadow, and padding.
- **AC:** Each card type shown. Consistent 12px border radius, 16px padding. Subtle shadow on LG, flat with border on XS/SM.

### L2-9.7: Loading & Empty States
Design skeleton loaders, spinner, and empty state illustrations for no data, no scans, and error scenarios.
- **AC:** Skeleton loaders match the shape of content they replace. Spinner is centered. Empty states have illustration, title, description, and CTA button.

## L1-10: Clinical Partner Portal

### L2-10.1: Patient List / Dashboard
Design a clinician dashboard with patient list table, search/filter bar, and summary stats (total patients, flagged cases, pending referrals).
- **AC:** Data table on MD/LG with columns: name, last scan date, redness score, status, actions. Card list on XS/SM. Search bar and filter chips at top. Summary stat cards above table.

### L2-10.2: Patient Detail View
Design a patient detail screen showing longitudinal eye health data, scan history gallery, AI diagnostic summaries, and treatment timeline.
- **AC:** Tabbed sections: Overview, Scans, Diagnosis, Treatment. On XS/SM: tabs as horizontal scroll. On MD/LG: tabs as top bar. Scan gallery is grid of thumbnails.

### L2-10.3: Referral Management
Design a referral inbox showing incoming AI-escalated cases with urgency level, patient summary, and accept/decline actions.
- **AC:** List view with urgency color coding (red=urgent, yellow=standard). Each item: patient name, condition, urgency badge, timestamp, and action buttons. Expandable detail on tap.
